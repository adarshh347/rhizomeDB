The "Leap" — Why Heidegger warns you first
We are trying to learn thinking.
The path is long.
At some point only a leap can take us further.
Normally when we learn something, knowledge accumulates:

* Lesson 1 → Lesson 2 → Lesson 3
* Add facts
* Build concepts
This is a gradual movement.
But Heidegger says genuine thinking doesn't work like that.
understanding happens through a jump in perspective.(anch)
(rev)
You stop seeing language as words and start seeing it as a world-making force.
You stop seeing technology as machines and start seeing it as a way reality gets revealed.
You stop seeing yourself as a thing and start seeing yourself as a being-in-the-world.
(/rev)

No amount of fact collection automatically produces that.
You suddenly see differently.
That's the leap.

7. Why he suddenly talks about hands
This part looks weird until you see what he's doing.
He says:
Apes can grasp.
But apes do not have hands.
Biologically they do.
So what does he mean?
He's talking about essence, not anatomy.
The human hand:

* writes
* blesses
* greets
* points
* creates art
* signs
The hand belongs to language.
The hand belongs to meaning.
The hand belongs to thought.
That's why he says:
Only a being that speaks can truly have hands.
The hand is not merely a tool.
It is an expression of thinking.
</des>
